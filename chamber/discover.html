History of Cache Valley

Cache Valley, located in Northern Utah and extending into Idaho, has a rich history dating back to its discovery by fur trapper Osbourne Russell in 1833. Its fertile lands attracted the pioneer settlers of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in the 1850s, leading to the development of farming communities. Today, it's known for its scenic beauty and agricultural prowess.

Farmer's Market

Experience the abundance of Cache Valley's Farmers' Market, taking place every Saturday throughout July and August. Taste the freshness of locally grown produce, artisanal goods, and handmade crafts while supporting our community of growers and makers.
